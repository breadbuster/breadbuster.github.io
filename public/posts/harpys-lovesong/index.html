Harpy’s Lovesong

2021-04-01 :: breadbuster
#multigrain  #rouge de bordeaux  #hard red winter wheat  #sungold spelt  #abruzzi rye  #carolina gold rice 

The first time I tasted naturally leavened bread was in April 2014, in Athens, Georgia. I was hitting a low point in life. A friend brought me a loaf of multigrain bread from Independent Baking Co., a new bakery in the heart of Five Points.

I didn’t have the faintest idea what sourdough was, but that loaf of bread – dark caramel-colored crackly crusts enrobed a cream-colored nutty crumb, with a slight tang and a satisfying flavor – cheered me up somehow and remained etched in my memory.

This bread is an attempt to recreate that magical warm moment.

Levain⌗

ingredient baker’s %
100g freshly milled brown teff (Desert Oasis, NV) 100%
100g whole milk 100%
stiff WW starter 15%

In case you didn’t know, teff is the world’s smallest “grain” (actually the seed of an Ethiopian grass) that’s mostly bran and germ.

Microwave freshly milled teff flour and milk for 2 mins, until a porridge consistency is reached. Add the starter after the mixture has cooled off completely. Leave the levain to mature for 14+ hours at ~65¬∞F.

Soaker⌗

Soak as many varieties of grains and seeds as you would like. Total dry weight is 40% of the flour weight in the final dough.

ingredient baker’s %
millet 10%
quinoa 10%
kaniwa 10%
amaranth 10%
pumpkin seeds 10%
rolled oats 10%
flaxseeds 10%
sunflower seeds 10%
poppy seeds 10%
sesame seeds 10%
water 150%

Let the soaker stand at room temperature for 12 hours.

Dough⌗

ingredient baker’s %
80% extraction wheat flour (Rouge de Bordeaux - Dayspring Farms, GA) 80%
whole spelt flour (Sungold spelt - Grapewood Farm, VA) 10%
whole rye flour (Abruzzi rye - Carter Farms, NC) 5%
brown rice flour (Charleston Gold - Anson Mills, SC) 5%
water 85%
salt 2%
soaker (not drained) 100%
shredded coconut (for dusting) 2%-5%

Cold-ferment for 24+ hours.
