Breadford von Regrainton

2019-12-09 :: breadbuster
#glenn  #hard red spring wheat  #single variety  #whole wheat  #california  #brewers spent grains  #papaya seeds  #zero waste  #unsifted 

I picked up a bag of Glenn wheat berries from Kenter Canyon Farms at the Atwater Village Farmers' Market in Los Angeles. It cost ~$2/lb, not cheap but not ridiculously overpriced either. The Glenn wheat was grown on Kenter Canyon Farms in Ventura County, along the south side of the Santa Clara River.

Glenn is a modern HRSW developed by a NDSU breeding program to provide better scab resistance and higher yield. It was released by the North Dakota Agricultural Experiment Station in the spring of 2005. It has a fairly high protein content of 13%-14%. I have seen quite a few local and regional mills across the country label Glenn as a “heritage” wheat – very curious.

I had long wanted to bake a treberbrot, a hearty German bread made with the addition of spent grains from brewing beers. The spent grain adds some sweetness, texture and a lot of fiber to the bread.

First, I milled the Glenn wheat berries, and sifted half into a 80% extraction flour, leaving the other half wholegrain (100% extraction).

Next, I built a levain with

  • sifted-out wheat bran; and
  • a smooth pur√©e of spent grains (from brewing Berliner Weiss) and boiled jackfruit seeds.

To make the dough stronger, I decided to use a 2-dough lamination method.

Dough #1:

ingredient baker’s %
80% extraction wheat flour (Glenn - Kenter Canyon Farms, CA) 100%
h2o 85%
bran levain 40%
salt 2%

This will be the stronger half of the dough that lifts the whole bread. You can totally swap water for beer here.

Dough #2:

ingredient baker’s %
whole wheat flour (Glenn - Kenter Canyon Farms, CA) 100%
h2o 70%
spent grain pur√©e 60%
bran levain 20%
salt 2%
toasted flaxseeds 5%

I mixed 1 part spent grains to 1 part water, and pur√©ed the mixture in a blender. Then I autolyzed the flour, along with the flaxseeds, in this thick grainy smoothie diluted with water.

With all of the wheat bran added back to the dough through the levain, this bread retains its whole-wheat integrity.

Add-ins:

ingredient baker’s %
spent grains 10%
sunflower seeds 5%
roasted papaya seeds, ground 5%
raisins 5%

Roasted papaya seeds have a dark cocoa brown color and smell deeply aromatic. They taste like peppercorns.

I did 4 folds with each dough during the first 3 hours. Then I combined both doughs by gently stretching them out into big rectangles and placing dough #2 on top of dough #1. I sprinkled the add-ins during lamination.

The dough was bulk-fermented for 5 hours in total at room temperature, shaped into a taut boule (dusted with grated coconut), and then retarded at 40¬∞F for 10 hours.

Next day, I let the dough warm up for a few hours until ~100% risen. I baked it on a shitty pizza pan in my ghetto toaster oven.

It grew so big during baking that it nearly filled up every cubic inch of the toaster oven!

The crust came out crispy and thin. The crumb is so freakin' airy.

The taste is deeply flavorful with a mild tang and a subtle sweetness from spent grains and raisins.

Superbly delightful.

NGL, it tasted even better the next day.
