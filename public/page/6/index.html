Welcome!

I’m breadbuster, an engineer, yeast whisperer, and open-source baker.

I specialize in slow breads handmade from freshly milled grains and wild yeasts.

I promote GYOG (grind your own grains) and BYOB (bake your own breads).

I salute open-source seeds and grain diversity.

I bake through the lens of economics, finance, history, politics, and the rise and fall of nations.

I hope to build a decentralized, equitable, and resilient food system.

This breadlog is a husk-to-endosperm journey exploring small-batch, unique grains with interesting terroir. It’s also an invitation to the greater community project of changing our relationships with nature and food.

I’m currently experimenting with craft beer yeasts.

Feel free to hang around. Scroll down to see my recent bread-plays. Explore my unsifted heresies. Take a peek at my California grain adventures.
